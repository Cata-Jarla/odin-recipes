Cheesecake

Description

Chessecake is a decant dessert known for its luscious, smooth filling and delicate balance of sweet and tangy flavors. his dessert is highly versatile and can be customised with various additions and toppings. From classic fruit sauces to indulgent layers of chocolate, caramel, or even matcha, cheesecake adapts to a wide range of tastes.

It can be served chilled or slightly warm, depending on the style, and works just as well in elegant dinners as it does in casual gatherings. Its smooth texture and bold flavour make cheesecake a dessert that leaves a lasting impression. Cheesecake's history stretches back thousands of years, originating in ancient Greece where it was served to athletes during the first Olympic Games.

Ingredients

  • 200g digestive biscuits or graham crackers, crushed
  • 100g unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 tbsp sugar
  • 500g cream cheese, softned
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • zest of half lemon
  • 300g fresh or frozen strawberries

    • Steps

    1. Preheat the oven to 170C.
    2. In a bowl, mix crushed biscuits with melted butter and optional sugar until combined.
    3. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 20-23 cm springform pan.
    4. Bake for 10 minutes.
    5. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth.
    6. Add sugar, sour cream, vanilla and lemon zest. Mix until creamy and well blended.
    7. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing gently after each addition. Avoid overmixing to prevent cracks.
    8. Pour the filling over the cooled crust and smooth the top.
    9. Wrap the outside of the springform pan with foil and place it in a larger baking tray.
    10. Fill the outer tray with hot water to abouth halfwayup the sides of the pan.
    11. Bake for 50-60 minutes, until the edges are set and the center slightly jiggles.
    12. Turn off the oven, leave the cheesecake inside with the door slightly open for 1 hour.
    13. Remove from the oven, cool to room temperature.
    14. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results.
    15. In a saucepan, combine strawberries, sugar and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat until the strawberries release juices and soften - 10 minutes.
    16. For a thicker sauce, stir in cornstarch mixed with water, and cook for another 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.
    17. Let it cool, then spoon over the cilled cheesecake.
    18. Slice and serve cold. Optional: garnish with fresh strawberries or whipped cream.
