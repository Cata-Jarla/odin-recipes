Romanian Sarmale

Description

Sarmale is a beloved traditional dish across Eastern Europe and the Balkans, especially famous in Romania, Moldova, Bulgaria, Serbia and Turkey. Each country has its own version, but the essence remains the same: minced meat and rice wrapped in cabbage or vine leaves, simmered slowly in a rich tomato-based sauce.

Sarmale - Romanian Cultural Background - Sarmale is a national dish of Romania, especially Christmas, Easter and weddings. It originates from Ottoman Empire, which influenced the romanian cuisine during centuries of interaction. Romanians adopted the dish by using pickled cabbage instead of vine leaves and adding smoked meats and spices for deeper flavor.

Ingredients

    Make 50 rolls
  • 1kg ground pork
  • 100g rice
  • 2 medium onions
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1 bunch dill
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large pickled cabbage
  • 500ml tomato juice or passata
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 200g smoked bacon or smoked ribs
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • fresh ground pepper

Steps

  1. Rinse the pickled cabbage, if too salty or sour.
  2. Carefully remove leave and trim thick veins with knife.
  3. Saute chopped onions in oil until soft.
  4. Mix the onions with ground meat, rice, parpika, dill, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  5. Place a spoonfull of filling on a cabbage leaf.
  6. Fold the sides over and roll tightly like a burrito.
  7. Tuck in the ends so the filling stays sealed.
  8. At the bottom of a large pot, place some chopped cabbage or leftover mall leaves, smoked bacon/ribs if using.
  9. Arrange sarmale in circular layers.
  10. Add more chopped cabbage between layers if desired.
  11. Mix tomato juice, tomato paste, water/broth, bay leaves, thyme, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  12. Pour over the rolls until almost covered.
  13. Cover and cook on low heat for 2.5-3 hours
  14. Alternatively, you can bake them in the oven at 160° for the same amount of time.
  15. Add water if needed during cooking.
  16. Serve hot with mamaliga, and a dollop of sour cream.
  17. Offten accompanied by hot peppers or crusty bread.
