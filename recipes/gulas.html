Gulas

Description

Gulas is a hearty, flavorful stew or soup that originates from Hungary, but it's also beloved across Central and Eastern Eurpe, including Romania, Slovakia, Austria and Serbia. It's especially known for its rich paprika-flavored broth and tender chunks of meat.

Cultural Background of Gulas- The word gulyas in Hungarian means "herdsman" - the dish was traditionally cooked by cattle herders over open fires in the Great Hungarin Plain. It started as a simple soup made with meat, onions, and paprika. Over time, it evolved into different regional styles: thick stews, soups or even versions with dumplings or pasta.

Ingredients

    Serves 4-6
  • 800g beef chunk or shank
  • 2 large onions
  • 3 tbsp sunflower oil or lard
  • 2 tbsp sweet parpika
  • 1 tsp caraway seeds
  • 2-3 garlic cloves
  • 2 medium carrots
  • 1 parsnip
  • 1-2 patatoes
  • 1 small bell pepper
  • 1 medium tomato
  • 1.5-2L beef broth or water
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: csipetke(small Hungarian pinched pasta)

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat oil or lard.
  2. Add chopped onions and saute until golden.
  3. Remove from heat, stir in paprika.
  4. Immediately add the meat cubes and stir to coat in paprika and onions.
  5. Return to heat and cook until the meat is browned on all sides.
  6. Add garlic, caraway seeds, choppe tomato, bell pepper, carrots and parsnip.
  7. Stir well, then add enough broth or water to cover everything.
  8. COver and simmer gently for 1.5-2 hours, until meat is tender.
  9. Add diced potatoes about 30 minutes before the end of cooking.
  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Serve hot in deep bowls accompanied by crusty bread or rustic rolls. Some add a spoonful of sour cream on top.
  12. Best enjoyed the next day - the flavors deepen beautifully.
